### Introduction

The Centre for Research and Integrated Development (CeRID) is a non-profit organization founded in

1.  It operates in several regions of Somalia, with a head office in Beled-Hawo district of Gedo\
    region. CeRID promotes community self-reliance for recovery and development through knowledgebased initiatives. Its thematic focus programs include: Research and Analysis, Education and Skills\
    Development, Environment and Natural Resource Management, Economic Empowerment and\
    Livelihoods, Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), Peace-building and Conflict Transformation,\
    Gender Equity and Women Empowerment.\
    CeRID is currently implementing BHA Somalia Multi-sector Emergency Response Project in Luuq and\
    Qansahdere, therefore it is looking for a qualified Community Nutrition & Hygiene Officer at CeRID\
    office in Qansahdere.

Summary of the Post\
Under the directives of the Program Manager, the Community Nutrition Officer will be responsible for\
the day-to-day management of the Community Nutrition sector. The officer helps the Program Manager\
in developing the Community Nutrition department initiatives focusing on: building community\
Nutrition approaches for the promotion of good nutrition and health standards.\
The officer shall be responsible to perform a wide variety of tasks to ensure effective and efficient\
management of the Community Nutrition sector. The officer shall be required to assess the gaps in the\
sector, design, implement, report and documentation of the Community Nutrition sector initiatives and\
activities. Working with the program team under the leadership of the Program Manager, the position\
also contributes to the development and implementation of organizational strategies on the Community\
Nutrition department.

Key Responsibilities and Duties;

- Carry out community health sensitization, mobilization and awareness on the project objectives, the approach and the community accountability and feedback mechanisms in place
- Objectives, the approach and the community accountability and feedback mechanisms in Sensitize project beneficiaries, raising awareness on nutrition, including empowerment of beneficiaries to make nutrition conscious purchases during voucher redemption based on Food groups (vitamins, carbohydrates, proteins, oils)
- Identify and work with Community Nutrition volunteers from project beneficiaries to support in the successful implementation of the project nutritional messaging and practices
- Select mothers/women with help of CNVs from the already selected beneficiaries who portray and show good nutrition practices to act as models for other families. (pick nutritious foods for the family from vendors, take their children for vaccinations, adhere to ICYF practices, attend ANCs, etc). These will be selected from discussions with communities who will select women who they deem fits this criteria
- Support Community Nutrition volunteers on the cooking demonstration on food preparation to avoid nutrient losses during preparation
- Support Community Nutrition volunteers to Promote health, nutrition and hygiene within the home environment
- Facilitate IYCF promotion within the community
- Follow up beneficiaries who have challenges redeeming their vouchers
- Advice and train the communities can optimise their performance by educating them on the type of food they should redeem and eat more.
- Come up with training modules for Communities which clearly states ways and means they can improve their nutritional level and also foods they need to eat to reduce the chances of communicable diseases.
- Document procedures, results and conclusions of the project activities
- Prepare regularly and effectively the project monthly, quarterly and end native reports using the donor guidelines and standards
- Work closely with donor by providing, information, updates and other write ups they might require from time to time
- Attend relevant meeting organized by different organizations with different capacities and contribute the meetings accordingly
- Work closely with other project partners, communities' leaders and relevant country, regional and district level authorities on community nutrition improvements and concerns.
- Work closely with the Program Manager and other project team to ensure that the program/project reports are prepared in a timely manner for submission.
- Perform any other task assigned by the supervisor

Qualifications

- Bachelor degree in Community Nutrition, Public Health, Food & Nutrition, Nursing or equivalent field from a recognized institution.

Work Experience

- At least 3 years of work experience in emergency nutrition work and implementation of feeding programmes, including monitoring and evaluation
- Work experience in Bay region Somalia
- Ability to speak Maay Dialect and interact with people

- Experience in nutrition assessments and understanding of nutritional surveillance and information systems.
- Excellent communication skills and a willingness to be respectful, kind, sensitive and empathise with all beneficiaries and their careers
- Willingness and capacity to be flexible and accommodating when faced with difficult and frustrating working conditions

- Excellent communication skills both verbal and written in English language.
- Excellent interpersonal skills and a collaborative management style.
- A demonstrated commitment to high professional ethical standards in a diverse workplace.
- Open to direction and collaborative work style and commitment to get the job done.
